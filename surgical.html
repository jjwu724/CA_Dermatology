Surgical Dermatology

Your skin protects you every day, but when conditions like growths, cancers, or persistent lesions arise, surgical expertise becomes essential. Our skilled dermatologists specialize in precise, effective procedures to address these concerns, blending advanced techniques with compassionate care to restore your skin's health and your peace of mind.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

The most common form of skin cancer, appearing as a pearly or waxy bump, often on sun-exposed areas like the face or neck. It grows slowly but can invade nearby tissues if untreated. Treatments include surgical excision, Mohs surgery, or cryotherapy for smaller lesions.


Cysts

Fluid-filled or semi-solid sacs beneath the skin, often painless but sometimes tender or inflamed, commonly on the face, neck, or trunk. Treatments involve surgical excision, drainage, or corticosteroid injections to reduce inflammation.


Melanoma

A dangerous skin cancer arising from pigment-producing cells, often presenting as an asymmetrical, irregularly colored mole that changes over time. Early detection is critical. Treatments include wide surgical excision, immunotherapy, or radiation for advanced cases.


Mole Removal

A procedure to remove benign or suspicious moles for cosmetic reasons or cancer concerns, typically on the face or body. Techniques vary based on the mole’s nature. Treatments include shave excision, surgical excision, or laser removal for select cases.


Skin Cancer

A broad term for abnormal skin cell growth, including basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and melanoma, often caused by UV damage. Treatments depend on type and stage, ranging from surgical removal and cryotherapy to chemotherapy or immunotherapy.


Squamous Cell Carcinoma

A skin cancer forming in the outer layers, often as a scaly, red patch or sore that doesn’t heal, linked to sun exposure. It can spread if untreated. Treatments include Mohs surgery, excision, or radiation therapy for deeper cases.
