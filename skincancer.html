Skin Cancer

Skin cancer is the most common form of cancer in the United States, but with early intervention and proper care, it is highly treatable. From surgical solutions to advanced radiation therapies, our team is ready to act with expertise and precision to protect your skin and restore your peace of mind.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

The most common form of skin cancer, appearing as a pearly or waxy bump, often on sun-exposed areas like the face or neck. It grows slowly but can invade nearby tissues if untreated. Treatments include surgical excision, Mohs surgery, or cryotherapy for smaller lesions.


Melanoma

A dangerous skin cancer arising from pigment-producing cells, often presenting as an asymmetrical, irregularly colored mole that changes over time. Early detection is critical. Treatments include wide surgical excision, immunotherapy, or radiation for advanced cases.


Squamous Cell Carcinoma

A skin cancer forming in the outer layers, often as a scaly, red patch or sore that doesn't heal, linked to sun exposure. It can spread if untreated. Treatments include Mohs surgery, excision, or radiation therapy for deeper cases.

Curettage and Desiccation

A procedure that scrapes away abnormal skin growths with a scoop-like tool, then uses an electric current to dry and destroy remaining cancer cells. It's commonly used for small, shallow skin cancers. The process is straightforward and typically done in a single visit.


Excision

A surgical technique that removes skin cancer by cutting it out along with a small margin of surrounding healthy tissue to ensure it's fully gone. It's often applied to larger or deeper growths. Stitches are used to close the area afterward.


Mohs Surgery

A method that removes skin cancer layer by layer, checking each under a microscope until no cancer remains, sparing as much healthy skin as possible. It's suited for cancers in visible or sensitive areas. The approach aims to balance thoroughness with minimal impact.


Superficial Radiation Therapy

A non-invasive treatment using low-energy X-rays to target and destroy abnormal skin cells, often for early-stage cancers or precancerous spots. It's precise, sparing healthy tissue, and ideal for areas like the face or neck. Sessions are quick, painless, and require no cutting or downtime.
