Medical Dermatology

Your skin is your body's first line of defense, but it's vulnerable to a wide range of conditions caused by genetics, environmental irritants, infections, and even the sun. From acne and eczema to skin cancer and hair loss, our expert dermatology team is here to diagnose and treat all concerns affecting your skin, hair, and nails. With advanced techniques and personalized care, we're committed to helping you achieve healthier skin and lasting confidence, whatever the challenge.

Acne

A common skin condition characterized by clogged pores, pimples, blackheads, and sometimes cysts or nodules. It often occurs on the face, chest, or back and can be influenced by hormones, oil production, or bacteria. Treatments include topical creams, oral medications, or in-office procedures like chemical peels.


Actinic Keratosis

A rough, scaly patch on the skin caused by prolonged sun exposure. Often appearing on the face, scalp, or hands, it’s considered a precancerous condition that may develop into skin cancer if untreated. Treatments range from cryotherapy and topical medications to laser therapy or surgical removal.


Alopecia Areata

An autoimmune disorder that leads to sudden, patchy hair loss on the scalp or other areas of the body. It occurs when the immune system attacks hair follicles, resulting in round bald spots. Treatments may include corticosteroid injections, topical immunotherapy, or minoxidil to stimulate hair regrowth.


Atopic Dermatitis

A chronic, itchy skin condition often linked to allergies or a family history of eczema, asthma, or hay fever. It typically causes red, inflamed patches, commonly on the face, arms, or legs. Treatments include moisturizers, topical steroids, or antihistamines to relieve itching and inflammation.


Contact Dermatitis

A skin reaction caused by direct contact with an irritant or allergen, resulting in red, itchy, or blistered patches. It can occur anywhere on the body exposed to substances like soaps, plants, or metals. Treatments involve avoiding triggers, using corticosteroid creams, or applying soothing ointments.


Eczema (Dermatitis)

A general term for inflammatory skin conditions that cause dry, red, and itchy patches, often worsened by scratching. It can stem from various triggers like stress or environmental factors. Treatments range from emollients and topical medications to phototherapy for severe cases.


Fungal Infections (Athlete's Foot, Jock Itch)

Skin conditions caused by fungi thriving in warm, moist areas, leading to itchy, red, and scaly rashes. Athlete’s Foot affects the feet, while Jock Itch targets the groin. Treatments include antifungal creams, powders, or oral medications for persistent cases.


Hair Loss (Alopecia)

A condition involving partial or complete hair loss, often due to genetics, stress, or underlying health issues. It can affect the scalp or other body areas in patterns or patches. Treatments may involve minoxidil, corticosteroids, or hair transplant procedures.


Hidradenitis Suppurativa

A chronic inflammatory disorder causing painful, boil-like lumps under the skin, typically in areas like the armpits or groin. It results from blocked hair follicles and can lead to scarring. Treatments range from antibiotics and anti-inflammatory drugs to surgical drainage or laser therapy.


Hives (Urticaria)

Raised, itchy welts on the skin caused by allergic reactions, stress, or infections. They can appear suddenly and vary in size, often fading within hours. Treatments include antihistamines, avoiding triggers, or corticosteroids for severe cases.


Hyperhidrosis (Excessive Sweating)

A condition marked by excessive sweating beyond what’s needed to regulate body temperature, often affecting the hands, feet, or underarms. Treatments range from prescription antiperspirants and Botox injections to iontophoresis or surgery.


Itching (Pruritus)

An uncomfortable sensation prompting the urge to scratch, often linked to dry skin, allergies, or underlying conditions. It can occur anywhere on the body. Treatments include moisturizers, topical steroids, or antihistamines to reduce irritation.


Melasma

A skin condition causing brown or grayish patches, typically on the face, due to sun exposure, hormones, or genetics. It’s more common in women. Treatments involve sunscreen, hydroquinone creams, or chemical peels to lighten pigmentation.


Moles

Common skin growths, usually benign, appearing as small, pigmented spots from clustered melanocytes. They can change over time, raising cancer concerns. Treatments include monitoring, surgical removal, or biopsy if suspicious.


Molluscum Contagiosum

A viral infection producing small, painless, pearly bumps with a central dimple, often on the face, trunk, or limbs. It’s contagious but usually resolves on its own. Treatments may include cryotherapy, topical agents, or curettage for faster clearance.


Nail Conditions

Disorders affecting the nails, such as fungal infections, brittleness, or discoloration, often due to trauma, infection, or systemic issues. Treatments vary from antifungal medications and nail care to addressing underlying health problems.


Psoriasis

A chronic autoimmune condition causing red, scaly patches from rapid skin cell buildup, commonly on the elbows, knees, or scalp. Treatments include topical steroids, phototherapy, or systemic medications to manage symptoms.


Rashes

Generalized skin eruptions varying in appearance (red, bumpy, or scaly) due to allergies, infections, or irritants. They can occur anywhere on the body. Treatments depend on the cause, including creams, antihistamines, or avoiding triggers.


Ringworm

A fungal infection causing a red, itchy, ring-shaped rash, often on the skin or scalp. Despite the name, it’s not caused by worms and is highly contagious. Treatments include antifungal creams, oral medications, or medicated shampoos.


Rosacea

A chronic condition leading to facial redness, flushing, and sometimes pimples or visible blood vessels, often triggered by heat, stress, or diet. Treatments range from topical gels and oral antibiotics to laser therapy for redness.


Seborrheic Dermatitis

A common condition causing red, flaky, and itchy skin, often on the scalp (dandruff), face, or chest, linked to oiliness or yeast. Treatments include medicated shampoos, antifungal creams, or corticosteroids.


Shingles

A painful, blistering rash caused by the reactivation of the varicella-zoster virus (chickenpox), typically on one side of the body. Treatments involve antiviral drugs, pain relief, and soothing lotions to speed recovery.


Skin Tags

Small, soft, benign growths that hang off the skin, often in areas like the neck or armpits, due to friction or genetics. Treatments include surgical removal, cryotherapy, or tying off with a thread.


Vitiligo

A condition where patches of skin lose pigment, turning white, due to the destruction of melanocytes, possibly autoimmune-related. Treatments may include topical steroids, phototherapy, or skin camouflage to even tone.


Warts

Rough, contagious growths caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV), appearing on hands, feet, or elsewhere. Treatments range from salicylic acid and cryotherapy to laser removal for stubborn cases.
